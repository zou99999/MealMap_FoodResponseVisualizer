Why Sugar, Protein, and Calories?

Sugar, protein, and calorie content are key factors that influence glucose response: sugar typically drives sharp spikes, protein can help stabilize it, and total calories provide context.

By connecting these nutrients to glucose trends, we give users a clearer picture of how different meals affect their energy, focus, and long-term health.

Why Glucose?

Glucose is one of the most revealing signals your body gives you. When we eat, our bodies break food into nutrients — and carbohydrates become glucose, a form of sugar that fuels everything from your brain to your muscles.

Some meals trigger rapid spikes and crashes, leaving you tired, hungry, or unfocused. Others give steady energy, less craving, better mood. By visualizing glucose trends, we get a real-time window into how food affects us.

Everyone’s glucose response is different — depending time of day, gut microbiome, stress, sleep, and even food combinations. We are not judging what food is "bad", only exploring: How does this meal tend to affect the body — and could it affect you similarly?
